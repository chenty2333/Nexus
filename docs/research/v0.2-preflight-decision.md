# v0.2 research preflight decision

Decision date: 2026-07-14

Decision: **narrow-go**

Nexus will proceed with the fixed production-identity experiment in
[RFC 0001](../rfcs/0001-production-identity.md), but it will not treat any
individual ingredient as a contribution and will not use novelty or firstness
wording. “Go” authorizes running the falsifiable experiment; “narrow” is the
claim boundary, not a forecast that the final result will be positive.

## Basis

- The Stage 7B comparison now has 15 primary full-text audits and one honestly
  unresolved metadata-only row, Atomic RPC. The detailed search and comparison
  axes are recorded in
  [the related-work preflight](related-work-preflight.md).
- The corrected Shadow Drivers paper already establishes driver restart,
  request and configuration tracking, recovery-time proxying, resource
  reattachment, IRQ and DMA fencing where supported, and explicit
  post-submission ambiguity. CSER therefore cannot claim those mechanisms
  separately.
- The remaining question is a composition question: whether one real
  workload-created causal authority identity and one conserved ledger can span
  personality, filesystem, block, device, and DMA ownership across restart and
  root revocation, then return an honest result after device commit.
- Atomic RPC remains a live refutation candidate. Until its primary full text
  is audited, the related-work layer cannot authorize a stronger contribution
  verdict.

## Selected experiment

The accepted workload is one digest-bound static Linux guest reading fixed
bytes through a same-boot VirtIO block request. The mandatory evidence phases
remain separate:

1. successor TLA+ semantics and an independent safe-Rust oracle;
2. registry-native service bindings, one root ledger, and identity-preserving
   execution through the real filesystem request to block preparation;
3. same-boot VirtIO/IOMMU ownership and the frozen shared-root fault matrix;
4. real OSTD lock, interrupt, and deterministic 2-vCPU and 4-vCPU execution;
5. predeclared baselines, raw measurements, related-work rerun, and the final
   `support-bounded`, `narrow`, or `reject` decision.

An intermediate phase may be published only as prospective or development
evidence. It cannot inherit a later phase's device, SMP, performance, or
contribution claim.

## Bounded implementation checkpoint

As of 2026-07-16, a focused one-vCPU OSTD/QEMU filesystem slice observes one
real user-mode fsd-v1 page-fault crash after the filesystem effect is prepared
but before device enrollment or commit. Before that crash, v1 queues a typed
delayed `Prepare` containing the complete `TaskKey` derived from its current OSTD
`Task` and its old `PortalHandle`. Only after the v1 completion waiter returns
and the protocol is confirmed `Crashed` does the slice construct the v2
`VmSpace`, completion waiter/waker, and OSTD `Task`. Fresh fsd-v2 then performs
Snapshot, Ready, Rebind, and explicit Adopt of the same effect. Post-adopt v2
only triggers delivery of the already-queued v1 command: old sender plus old
handle is rejected as `StaleBinding`, and old sender plus the adopted handle as
`NoSupervisor`, without changing the Registry failure-atomic projection. Only
then does the normal lane enroll the exact six-effect cohort and execute
same-boot VirtIO/IOMMU DMA. A paired pre-commit lane instead lets revoke win
before device publication. Both publish one guest result through polling with
PCI INTx masked.

This is a bounded implementation observation, not a revision of the
`narrow-go` decision or an RFC phase-exit claim. The post-device-commit/
pre-reply service crash, the remaining frozen fault cells, a real IRQ path,
2-vCPU and 4-vCPU execution, all filesystem fault paths, measurements, and the
final contribution decision remain open.

## Stop and pivot triggers

The implementation will stop or narrow further if any RFC kill condition is
observed, especially if the real path requires a synthetic replacement cohort,
cannot preserve one effect identity through device retry, cannot return an
honest post-commit result, or repeatedly leaks/resurrects ownership under the
2-vCPU and 4-vCPU schedules. A primary predecessor that already combines the
same fixed identity, fencing, ledger, commit/revoke, and device-tombstone
properties also forces a contribution reassessment.

This decision does not alter the immutable `v0.1.0` tag, DOI, artifacts, or
released claim ledger.
